Privacy Policy

App Freezer

Last updated: [2025-12-24]

This Privacy Policy explains how App Freezer ("we", "our", or "the app") handles user information and protects your privacy when you use our Android application.

1. Information We Collect

We respect your privacy. App Freezer does not collect, store, or sell any personal data.

The app may collect anonymous, non-personal data for the following purposes:

  • Crash and performance reports using Google Crashlytics or Google Analytics to improve app stability.
  • Advertising ID (ADS_ID), if ads are enabled, used only for advertising or analytics purposes.

All collected data is anonymous and cannot be used to identify individual users.

2. Permissions & Special Access

App Freezer requires certain permissions to provide its core functionality:

  • Device Administrator (BIND_DEVICE_ADMIN) (Optional)
    Required for freezing and unfreezing apps on non-root devices. This permission allows App Freezer to disable or enable applications only when explicitly requested by the user.
  • Root Access (Optional)
    Used only if granted by the user to enable advanced app management features on rooted devices.
  • WRITE_EXTERNAL_STORAGE
    Used to export and import XML backup files and to store backup data locally on your device.

App Freezer does not misuse permissions and does not access personal content such as messages, photos, contacts, or call logs.

3. Password & Security

If you enable password or biometric protection:

  • Authentication data is stored locally on your device only.
  • No passwords or biometric data are transmitted or stored on external servers.

4. Data Sharing

We do not sell, rent, or share personal data with third parties.

Anonymous analytics and crash data may be processed by trusted third-party services such as Google Crashlytics, according to their respective privacy policies.

5. Children’s Privacy

App Freezer is not intended for children under the age of 13 and does not knowingly collect personal information from children.

If you believe that a child has provided personal information, please contact us and we will take appropriate action.

6. User Responsibility & Disclaimer

Freezing system apps may cause device instability or unexpected behavior. You are solely responsible for how you use App Freezer.

We strongly recommend:

  • Backing up your data before freezing apps
  • Unfreezing all apps before Android system updates (OTA)
  • Freezing only apps you fully understand

7. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be posted on this page. You are encouraged to review this policy periodically.
